10 Mental Health Foundations to Support

Below are 10 mental health foundations that have been working to improve the lives of those suffering from mental illnesses and their families.

Please consider supporting or donating to them so that they can continue to help those in need.

The Jed Foundation

The Jed Foundation is a non-profit organization that works to prevent suicide among American teenagers and young adults. Founded in 2000 by a couple who lost one of their children to suicide, JED has been helping people across the country by helping schools create stronger substance abuse and suicide prevention programs, creating partnerships to make resources more accessible to teens and young adults, and more.

Brain & Behavior Research Foundation

Brain & Behavior Research Foundation is a group that funds scientific research in order to find cures for a variety of mental illnesses. To this date, the foundation has awarded hundreds of millions worth of research grants to scientists around the world. When they recieve donations, 100% is invested back into their research grants.

National Alliance on Mental Illness

National Alliance on Mental Illness is a foundation whose mission is to educate people on mental health and help improve the lives of people dealing with mental illnesses. They run public events and activities to increase education and fight the stigma around mental health.

Mental Health America

Mental Health America is a non-profit that provides various servies to help those suffering from a mental illness. Founded in 1909, MHA is the leading non-profit in the US focused on helping those with mental illnesses and promoting mental health in everyone. MHA provides education to Americans to help spark a conversation about Mental health and has many services to try and prevent mental illness.

American Foundation for Suicide Prevention

American Foundation for Suicide Prevention is a non-profit with chapters in all 50 states of the US that educates professionals, offers programs and resources for those at risk as well as survivors, and advocates for policies effecting suicide and prevention. Founded in 1987, AFSP dedicates itself to helping save lives and uplift those affected by suicide.

Child Mind Institute

Child Mind Institute is a non-profit focused on helping children, teenagers, and families struggling with mental illnesses or learning disorders. Child Mind Institute works to fulfil three commitments:giving the most effective clinical care possible, advancing science to help provide more improved diagnoses and treatments, and providing the most accurate information to families and communities.

Active Minds

Active Minds is a non-profit working to spread awareness about mental health and educate young adults. They empower young adults, to speak up about mental health through education, research, and advocacy. Active Minds was founded in 2003 and since then has become present in over 800 schools, with 550 being student-led chapters. Through these programs, more young adults become aware of mental health and are able to help reduce the stigma.

The Trevor Project

The Trevor Project is a foundation that has a focus of ending and preventing suicide among young adult members of the LGBTQ+ community. Founded in 1998, The Trevor Project participates in a large amount of advocacy work while also continuing to partake in research, publishing multiple original research papers.

Anxiety and Depression Association of America

Anxiety and Depression Association of America is an organization that focuses on preventing, treating, and curing anxiety, depression, and other related illnesses through education and research. ADAA runs special programs to break the stigma surrounding anxiety disorders, depression, and other related illnesses.

Shatterproof

Shatterproof is a non-profit that dedicates its work to the impact of substance abuse and mental illness on communities. This organization works to provide better addiction treatment, end the stigma around addiction, and advocate for policy changes that can save lives.
